## Project Choice

The first Chief of Staff project is to create a lightweight Launch Readiness Control Center for Drop 001.

This is the right first project because UNPROFITABLE is not blocked by more ideas. It is blocked by unclear readiness: product proof, fulfillment, finance inputs, checkout approval, and paid-traffic gating.

## Objective

Make it obvious, at a glance, what must be true before Jace approves checkout or paid traffic.

## Scope

### In scope

1. Define this week's top 3 launch priorities.
2. Separate Jace decisions from agent tasks.
3. List active blockers with owners.
4. Park do-later distractions.
5. Create follow-up prompts for the lane owners.
6. Keep Launch Decisions clean for approval-only items.

### Out of scope

1. New agents.
2. New product ideas.
3. Brand redesign.
4. Checkout launch.
5. Paid ad spend.
6. Final supplier/pricing/sample/policy approval.

## Current Priority Stack

1. **Product Proof**
   - Owner: Product / Supplier
   - Needed before checkout: blanks, GSM/fabric weight, material blend, fit/size chart, print/embroidery method, care/wash notes, sample proof requirements.

2. **Fulfillment Readiness**
   - Owner: Operations / CX
   - Needed before checkout: order flow, fulfillment owner, carrier/shipping regions, packaging, damaged-item process, return/exchange draft, support templates.

3. **Launch Math From Real Inputs**
   - Owner: Finance
   - Needed before inventory/ad approval: landed cost per item, margin per product/bundle, break-even units/revenue, upfront cash requirement, ad-spend risk, inventory risk.

## Decision Gates

Jace should not be asked to approve checkout or paid spend until these are prepared:

1. Supplier / blank recommendation.
2. Production-accurate sample proof process.
3. Fulfillment and shipping workflow.
4. Return/exchange/damaged-item policy.
5. COGS, landed cost, margin, and break-even model.
6. Brand QA review of any public claims.
7. Website QA with truthful CTAs and policy language.

## Agent Follow-Up Prompts

### Product / Supplier

Produce the Drop 001 Product Proof Checklist:

- Tee blank options, specs, size range, measurements, GSM/material, decoration method, care, sample path.
- Hoodie blank options, specs, size range, measurements, GSM/material, decoration method, care, sample path.
- Hat blank options, fit/closure, embroidery feasibility, material, sample path.
- Missing inputs that require Jace or supplier confirmation.

### Operations / CX

Draft the Launch Ops Readiness Sheet:

- Who fulfills.
- Carrier/options.
- Shipping regions.
- Packaging standard.
- Damaged-item flow.
- Return/exchange stance.
- Support templates.
- Launch-day checklist.

### Finance

Prepare the Launch Math Template waiting on supplier/ops inputs:

- COGS per item.
- Decoration cost.
- Packaging cost.
- Payment/platform fees.
- Shipping assumptions.
- Gross/contribution margin.
- Break-even units.
- Inventory quantity scenarios.
- Ad-spend risk and max acceptable CAC.

### Brand QA

Prepare the Claims Guardrail Review:

- Approved phrases.
- Banned phrases.
- Claims requiring proof.
- Product quality wording allowed only after Product/Supplier confirmation.

### Website Designer

Stand by for specs/policies before changing product pages. No new major direction until product proof and policy language are approved.

### Marketing

Prepare creative concepts only. Do not run paid traffic until product proof, landing path, claims, and finance guardrails are ready.

## What This Unlocks

Once these lane reports are complete, Jace can make clean approval decisions in Launch Decisions instead of being dragged into scattered agent chatter.

## Main Risk

The site and brand are ahead of product/ops proof. Launching checkout or paid traffic before proof would force the brand to make promises it may not be ready to support.

## Next Best Action

Product / Supplier should produce the Product Proof Checklist first, because Finance, Website, Brand QA, Ops, and Marketing all depend on those inputs.
