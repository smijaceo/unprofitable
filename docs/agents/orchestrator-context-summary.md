# UNPROFITABLE Orchestrator Context Summary

## 1. What UNPROFITABLE is

UNPROFITABLE is a premium black-and-white self-improvement streetwear and community brand with trading-origin authenticity. The brand posture is merch-first and community-second: disciplined, minimal, black/white, premium streetwear for builders, creators, operators, traders, and self-improvement-minded people turning setbacks into standards.

Core motto:

```text
UNPROFITABLE TODAY. PROFITABLE TOMORROW.
```

Current public copy standard:

```text
PROCESS OVER OUTCOME.
Blacked-out streetwear for people rebuilding their standards.
No signals. No guru act. No fake flex. Just a uniform for getting back to work.
```

## 5. What each existing agent currently owns

### Website Designer

Owns website layout, page flow, product/page presentation, mobile UX, CTA placement, trust/product-proof sections, drop-list conversion clarity, and customer-facing website copy clarity.

Current strength: very detailed site, asset, logo, mobile QA, product-photo, Shopify/static-site, and Drop 001 guidance.

Needs tightening: remind it not to own supplier decisions, finance, fulfillment, ad strategy, or product quality approval; make report format mandatory.

### Marketing

Owns paid/social campaign strategy, ad angles, hooks, UGC/creative prompts, Meta testing matrix, campaign goals, audience, CTA, and ad-to-landing-page match.

Current strength: strong campaign/funnel and paid-social guidance for Drop 001.

Needs tightening: marketing should create demand and send the right people to the right landing page. It should not become product QA, website owner, fulfillment owner, or finance decision-maker. Every creative should map to a specific campaign goal: drop-list signup, product proof, launch countdown, early access, or bundle/full-drop interest.

### Finance

Owns product costs, COGS, margins, pricing, budget, ad economics, cashflow, inventory risk, break-even, ROAS/CAC, and launch finance assumptions.

Current strength: already has strong UNPROFITABLE-only finance boundaries and excludes trading work.

Needs tightening: make supplier/product quality support explicit but not final approval; add required answers for upfront cost, landed cost, product/bundle margins, break-even revenue, required ROAS, inventory risk, and sensible price points; make report format mandatory.

## 6. What is missing

Missing lean launch agents:

1. Brand Director / Brand QA — protects identity, voice, visual rules, approved/banned language, public-facing copy, and creative review.
2. Product / Supplier — owns blanks, supplier comparison, GSM/fabric specs, fit/size chart, print/embroidery methods, samples, wash testing, product proof requirements.
3. Operations / Customer Experience — owns post-checkout order flow, fulfillment workflow, packaging, shipping, returns/exchanges, damaged-item process, support templates, FAQ, and launch-day operations checklist.

## Current biggest blockers

1. Final product specs are not confirmed: blanks, GSM/fabric weight, material blend, exact sizing, care, print/embroidery method, production timeline.
2. Fulfillment and CX details are not final: shipping carrier/cost/timeline, packaging, damaged-item flow, support email/templates, final return/exchange policy.
3. Checkout is not live: Shopify/import can exist, but products should remain draft/inventory `0` until product, finance, ops, policy, and test checkout are approved.
4. Paid ad traffic should wait until product proof, drop-list tracking, and landing-page message match are verified.
