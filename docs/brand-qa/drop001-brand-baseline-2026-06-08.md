# DROP 001 brand QA baseline

Date: 2026-06-08
Owner: Brand Director / Brand QA
Scope: public-facing UNPROFITABLE copy and brand-risk guardrails for DROP 001: DISCIPLINED / 001.

## First project

Build the baseline Brand QA file for Drop 001, then clean the highest-risk public copy that could overpromise product proof or drift into fake scarcity.

This is the right first project because the brand already has a strong site, but the risk is not the headline. The risk is small lines across product pages, shop cards, FAQs, and Shopify copy slowly turning into claims we cannot back yet.

## Brand position locked

UNPROFITABLE is black-and-white streetwear for people rebuilding their standards.

Streetwear first. Recovery arc second. Trading origin stays real, but restrained.

The brand should feel like:

- black garments
- white marks
- disciplined type
- quiet confidence
- product proof before checkout
- reset-day energy
- no fake flex

It should not feel like:

- a signals room
- a trading group
- a guru funnel
- crypto merch
- meme-store merch
- fake luxury
- hustle-poster motivation
- vague scarcity

## Watchlist phrases

These can work, but only with care:

- `limited first run` — use only after quantity is approved. Prefer `first production run` until then.
- `real sample photos` — use only when Product/Supplier confirms final samples. Prefer `product reference photos` or `sample proof will be posted before checkout`.
- `model references` — safe only when actual model info is confirmed. Otherwise say `fit references`.
- `first access has value` — safe, but avoid making early access sound like artificial scarcity.
- `bundle details` — safe while prices stay hidden, but do not imply discount or savings until Finance approves.

## Banned public directions

- guaranteed profits

## Copy QA decisions made

1. Product proof language must stay honest while checkout is closed.
   - If photos are references, call them references.
   - If samples are final, Product/Supplier can approve stronger wording.

2. Scarcity language stays restrained.
   - `First production run` is safer than `limited first run` until approved quantity is public.
   - `Restock is not guaranteed` is acceptable if paired with honest demand/production context.

3. Trading disclaimers are brand-safe and should stay.
   - `No signals. No guru act. No fake flex.` is approved.
   - Footer legal language blocks trading advice risk without killing the brand tone.

4. Product claims need proof before checkout.
   - Heavyweight, fabric weight, closure type, size chart, care instructions, and shipping promises need final supplier/ops confirmation.

## Initial audit findings

### Strong

- Homepage hero is on-brand: black/white, direct, disciplined.
- The no-guru language is clear without sounding defensive.
- Product proof before checkout is the right trust mechanism.
- The manifesto has a human voice and avoids generic motivational filler.
- The site mostly avoids money, charts, P&L, and trading-signal energy.

### Needs cleanup

- Some `real sample photos` / `real product angles` language may overclaim if current assets are product references rather than final sample proof.
- `limited first run` can read like scarcity marketing before quantity is approved.
- Product pages contain placeholder model references. Those should be replaced or verified before checkout.
- The Shopify CSV says `should be confirmed`; public Shopify descriptions should eventually become either confirmed facts or removed before launch.

## Pre-checkout Brand QA gate

Do not approve checkout until all are true:

- Final sample/product images are confirmed by Product/Supplier.
- Size chart and fit notes are real, not placeholder copy.
- Material, weight, print, embroidery, care, and closure details are confirmed.
- Shipping region and timeline are approved by Operations/CX.
- Return/exchange terms are approved by Operations/CX.
- Public price and bundle language are approved by Finance/Jace.
- No public page uses fake scarcity or unapproved quantity language.
- No page drifts into trading advice, signals, income claims, P&L, crypto, or guru framing.

## Suggested copy replacements

| Risky copy | Safer copy |
|---|---|
| Real sample photos and closeups | Product reference photos and closeups |
| Real product angles, closeups, fit references, and campaign shots | Product angles, detail closeups, fit references, and campaign shots |
| Limited first production run | First production run |
| Are these real photos? | Are these final sample photos? |
| The page uses Drop 001 product references and detail imagery. Final sample proof will be posted before checkout. | Current images are Drop 001 product references. Final sample proof will be posted before checkout. |

## Open decisions for Jace

1. Can we call any current product images `real sample photos`, or should all current images stay labeled as `product references`?
2. What exact first-run quantity can be public, if any?
3. Are the model heights/sizes on product pages real, or placeholders that need to be removed before checkout?
4. Does `The only flex is discipline` stay, or is `flex` too close to the culture we are blocking?

## Brand QA operating rule

If a line makes the brand sound more proven than it is, cut it down.

Better to sound disciplined than loud. Better to underclaim and show proof than write like every other drop trying to manufacture hype.
