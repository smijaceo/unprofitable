# DROP 001 Product/Supplier First Project — Agent Report

## Agent Report

**Agent:** Product / Supplier

**Current Goal:**
Create the first Product/Supplier operating project for Drop 001 and execute the first pass: audit current product proof, expose checkout blockers, and create a reusable Product Proof Gate.

**Top 3 Priorities:**
1. Lock the product proof standard before supplier/sample decisions drift.
2. Prevent unsupported website/marketing claims from outrunning actual specs.
3. Give Finance and Ops/CX the supplier/spec inputs they need for launch readiness.

**What Changed:**
- Created `docs/product/2026-06-08-drop001-product-proof-gate.md`.
- Audited current repo evidence from product pages, Shopify CSV/import notes, roadmap, blocker list, and Drop 001 image assets.
- Classified current product claims as approved, conditional, or not approved.
- Marked checkout as blocked from Product/Supplier until final blanks, materials, measurements, decoration methods, sample proof, care instructions, and wash-test evidence exist.

**What Is Blocked:**
- Final blank choice for tee, hoodie, and hat.
- GSM/fabric weight and material blend.
- Tee oversized/boxy proof via measurements.
- Hoodie heavyweight/structured hood proof.
- Hat crown/closure/head-size specs.
- Print/embroidery methods and durability proof.
- Shrinkage/care instructions.
- Real sample/product photos or clearly labeled production mockups.
- Supplier costs/MOQ/lead time needed by Finance.
- Production timing/care/damage rules needed by Ops/CX.

**What Needs Jace’s Decision:**
- Whether Product/Supplier should prioritize domestic premium blanks, local decorator/screen printer, POD/merch platform, or overseas cut-and-sew/small-batch sourcing for the first supplier shortlist.
- Final approval on blank choices and samples once options are presented.
- Final approval on whether claims like `premium`, `heavyweight`, `oversized`, and `structured` are allowed after proof is collected.
- Final size run and production quantity.
- Checkout going live.

**Next 3 Actions:**
1. Build a supplier/blank shortlist for the tee, hoodie, and hat against the proof gate.
2. Collect spec sheets/quote fields: blank cost, decoration cost, MOQ, lead time, size chart, GSM/blend, and sample cost.
3. Produce a Jace decision brief with 2-3 supplier paths and a recommended sample order.

**Risks / Concerns:**
- Current site/product pages look polished enough that customers may assume specs are final; keep checkout closed and claims cautious.
- Mockup/reference imagery must not be treated as real sample proof until origin is confirmed.
- `Heavyweight` and `oversized` are high-risk claims without actual GSM/measurement proof.
- Finance cannot do real margin math until supplier/decorator cost inputs exist.
